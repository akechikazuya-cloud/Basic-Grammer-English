ACTIVE AND PASSIVE VOICE QUIZ - PRACTICE EXERCISES
=====================================================
Instructions: All questions below are multiple choice, true/false, or 
matching. Just circle, underline, or write the letter/number of your 
answer. No full sentences needed!


SECTION 1: MULTIPLE CHOICE (ACTIVE OR PASSIVE?)
----------------------------------------------------
Choose whether each sentence is ACTIVE or PASSIVE voice.

1. "The chef prepared the meal."
   a) active      b) passive

2. "The meal was prepared by the chef."
   a) active      b) passive

3. "My phone was stolen."
   a) active      b) passive

4. "The committee approved the proposal."
   a) active      b) passive

5. "Dinner is served."
   a) active      b) passive


SECTION 2: MULTIPLE CHOICE (SPOT THE PASSIVE FORM)
----------------------------------------------------------
Choose the correctly formed passive voice sentence.

6. a) The city built a new library.
   b) A new library was built by the city.
   c) A new library build by the city.

7. a) The telephone invented over a century ago.
   b) The telephone was invented over a century ago.
   c) The telephone is invent over a century ago.

8. a) He was awarded first prize.
   b) He awarded first prize.
   c) He is award first prize.


SECTION 3: MATCHING (ACTIVE TO PASSIVE)
------------------------------------------
Match each active sentence on the left with its correct passive 
version on the right.

  1. "Maria baked a cake."               a) "A mistake was made in the report."
  2. "You made a mistake in the report."  b) "A cake was baked by Maria."
  3. "The teacher graded the essays."     c) "The essays were graded by the teacher."


SECTION 4: MULTIPLE CHOICE (WHY USE PASSIVE VOICE?)
------------------------------------------------------------
Choose the best reason passive voice is used in each sentence.

9. "The bridge is closed."
   a) Because the doer is unknown or not important
   b) Because the sentence needs to sound aggressive
   c) Because it must always include "by"

10. "Your feedback is valued."
    a) To sound blunt and direct
    b) To sound more polite and less like blaming someone
    c) Because there is no subject in the sentence

SECTION 6: MULTIPLE CHOICE (CONVERT TO PASSIVE)
------------------------------------------------------
Choose the correct passive version of each active sentence.

16. Active: "The government built a new bridge."
    a) A new bridge built by the government.
    b) A new bridge was built by the government.
    c) A new bridge is build by the government.

17. Active: "I recommend that you apply for this position."
    a) It recommended that you apply for this position.
    b) It is recommended that you apply for this position.
    c) It was recommend that you apply for this position.

18. Active: "The company laid off several employees."
    a) Several employees were laid off by the company.
    b) Several employees was laid off by the company.
    c) Several employees laid off by the company.


SECTION 7: WHICH VOICE FITS BEST?
------------------------------------------
Choose whether ACTIVE or PASSIVE voice fits best in each situation.

19. You know exactly who broke the vase and want to say so directly.
    a) active      b) passive

20. You don't know who left the door open, and it isn't important.
    a) active      b) passive

21. You want to sound polite instead of blaming a coworker directly 
    for a mistake.
    a) active      b) passive

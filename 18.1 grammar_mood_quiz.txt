===================================
INDICATIVE, IMPERATIVE, SUBJUNCTIVE MOOD QUIZ
===================================
Instructions: All questions below are multiple choice, true/false, or 
matching. Just circle, underline, or write the letter/number of your 
answer. No full sentences needed!


SECTION 1: MULTIPLE CHOICE (WHICH MOOD IS IT?)
----------------------------------------------------
Choose the correct mood for each sentence.

1. "We are meeting for lunch tomorrow."
   a) indicative     b) imperative     c) subjunctive

2. "Close the door, please."
   a) indicative     b) imperative     c) subjunctive

3. "If I were rich, I would travel the world."
   a) indicative     b) imperative     c) subjunctive

4. "The train leaves at 9 AM."
   a) indicative     b) imperative     c) subjunctive

5. "Don't forget your umbrella."
   a) indicative     b) imperative     c) subjunctive

6. "I wish I were taller."
   a) indicative     b) imperative     c) subjunctive


SECTION 2: MULTIPLE CHOICE ("WERE" VS. "WAS")
----------------------------------------------------
Choose the correct subjunctive form.

7. If I ____ you, I would apply for that job.
   a) was        b) were

8. She wishes she ____ more confident.
   a) was        b) were

9. If he ____ here, he would know what to do.
   a) was        b) were

10. I wish this project ____ finished already.
    a) was        b) were


SECTION 3: MULTIPLE CHOICE (DROPPING THE "-S")
--------------------------------------------------------
Choose the correct subjunctive verb form after trigger verbs.

11. The manager insists that everyone ____ on time.
    a) arrives     b) arrive

12. We suggest that he ____ his laptop.
    a) brings      b) bring

13. They requested that she ____ the form early.
    a) submits     b) submit

14. Our recommendation is that each student ____ the survey.
    a) does        b) do


SECTION 4: MATCHING (MOOD DEFINITIONS)
------------------------------------------
Match each mood on the left with its correct purpose on the right.

  1. Indicative        a) Imagines something unreal or hypothetical
  2. Imperative         b) States facts or real events
  3. Subjunctive        c) Gives commands or requests


SECTION 5: MATCHING (TRIGGER VERBS)
------------------------------------------
Which of these are common "trigger verbs" that call for the 
subjunctive mood? Match each word to YES or NO.

  1. insist          a) YES
  2. enjoy           b) NO
  3. suggest         c) YES
  4. walk            d) NO
  5. demand          e) YES
  6. request         f) YES


SECTION 6: TRUE OR FALSE
------------------------------
Read each sentence. Circle TRUE if it is grammatically correct, 
or FALSE if it is wrong.

15. "If I was you, I wouldn't buy a house."          TRUE / FALSE
16. "If I were you, I wouldn't buy a house."          TRUE / FALSE
17. "She recommended that each student take a note."  TRUE / FALSE
18. "She recommended that each student takes a note."  TRUE / FALSE
19. "I wish I was able to speak English fluently."     TRUE / FALSE
20. "I wish I were able to speak English fluently."    TRUE / FALSE


SECTION 7: WHICH MOOD FITS BEST?
------------------------------------------
Choose the mood that best fits each situation.

21. You want to state a simple fact about your daily schedule.
    a) indicative     b) imperative     c) subjunctive

22. You want to give your friend an instruction to hurry up.
    a) indicative     b) imperative     c) subjunctive

23. You want to imagine a situation that isn't currently true.
    a) indicative     b) imperative     c) subjunctive

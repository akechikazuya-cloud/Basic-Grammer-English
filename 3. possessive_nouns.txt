POSSESSIVE NOUNS
================

Possessive nouns show that something belongs to someone.


BASIC RULE: ADD AN APOSTROPHE + S ('s)
----------------------------------------
For most nouns, just add 's to show ownership.

  - Maria's umbrella
  - the dog's leash
  - Grandpa's watch
  - Mr. Lee's classroom


PLURAL NOUNS ENDING IN "S": ADD ONLY AN APOSTROPHE (')
---------------------------------------------------------
If the noun is already plural and ends in s, don't add another s —
just put the apostrophe after it.

  - the students' lockers
  - my neighbors' garden
  - the players' uniforms
  - the sisters' apartment

Tip: Say the word out loud. If it already sounds plural with an "s"
at the end, you only need the apostrophe — not an extra "s."


TWO PEOPLE WHO SHARE ONE THING: 'S GOES ON THE LAST NAME ONLY
------------------------------------------------------------------
When two people own the same thing together, only the second name
gets the 's.

  - Alex and Priya's business   (they own one business together)
  - Mom and Dad's car            (one shared car)
  - Liam and Noah's apartment    (they share one apartment)


TWO PEOPLE WHO OWN SEPARATE THINGS: 'S GOES ON BOTH NAMES
--------------------------------------------------------------
When each person owns their own separate item, both names need 's.

  - Anna's and Leo's phones     (Anna has her own phone, Leo has his own)
  - Maya's and Sam's backpacks  (two different backpacks)
  - Rita's and Omar's desks     (two different desks)


QUICK MEMORY TRICK
--------------------
Ask: "Is it one shared thing, or separate things?"

  - One shared thing   -> only the last name gets 's  -> Kim and Lee's dog
  - Separate things     -> both names get 's           -> Kim's and Lee's dogs

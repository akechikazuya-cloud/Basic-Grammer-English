INFINITIVES PART 2 QUIZ - PRACTICE EXERCISES
===================================
Instructions: All questions below are multiple choice, true/false, or 
matching. Just circle, underline, or write the letter/number of your 
answer. No full sentences needed!


SECTION 1: MULTIPLE CHOICE ("IT IS + INFINITIVE")
----------------------------------------------------------
Choose the correct infinitive form to complete each sentence.

1. It is time ____ .
   a) leave        b) leaving        c) to leave

2. It was a privilege ____ with such a talented team.
   a) work         b) working        c) to work

3. It is exciting ____ a new job.
   a) start        b) starting       c) to start

4. It was an honor ____ that award.
   a) receive      b) receiving      c) to receive


SECTION 2: MULTIPLE CHOICE (GERUND-ONLY VERBS)
----------------------------------------------------
Choose the correct form. Remember, these verbs only accept gerunds!

5. I enjoy ____ on the weekends.
   a) hike         b) hiking         c) to hike

6. She suggested ____ to the new restaurant.
   a) go           b) going          c) to go

7. He finally quit ____ last year.
   a) smoke        b) smoking        c) to smoke

8. Would you mind ____ the window?
   a) close        b) closing        c) to close


SECTION 3: MULTIPLE CHOICE (INFINITIVE-ONLY VERBS)
----------------------------------------------------------
Choose the correct form. Remember, these verbs only accept infinitives!

9. She decided ____ to a new city.
   a) move         b) moving         c) to move

10. He promised ____ me back.
    a) call         b) calling        c) to call

11. We can't afford ____ a new car right now.
    a) buy          b) buying         c) to buy

12. They refused ____ up.
    a) give         b) giving         c) to give


SECTION 4: MATCHING (GERUND ONLY VS. INFINITIVE ONLY)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Match each verb on the left with the correct group on the right.

  1. avoid         a) Infinitive only
  2. promise       b) Gerund only
  3. postpone       c) Infinitive only
  4. hope          d) Gerund only
  5. practice       e) Gerund only
  6. refuse        f) Infinitive only


SECTION 5: MULTIPLE CHOICE (EITHER, SAME MEANING)
----------------------------------------------------------
Choose the sentence that has the SAME meaning as the sentence given.

13. "She continued working."
    a) She continued to work.       b) She continued for working.

14. "I love to read."
    a) I love reading.               b) I love for reading.

15. "It started raining."
    a) It started to be rain.        b) It started to rain.


SECTION 6: MULTIPLE CHOICE (EITHER, DIFFERENT MEANING)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Choose the sentence that matches the meaning described.

16. Meaning: "I no longer text while driving; the habit ended."
    a) I stopped texting while driving.    b) I stopped to text while driving.

17. Meaning: "I have a memory of already locking the door."
    a) I remembered to lock the door.     b) I remember locking the door.

18. Meaning: "I made an effort, though I may not have succeeded."
    a) I tried exercising every morning.  b) I tried to exercise every morning.

19. Meaning: "I paused what I was doing in order to text my friend."
    a) I stopped texting my friend.       b) I stopped to text my friend.

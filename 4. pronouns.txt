PRONOUNS
========

A pronoun replaces a noun so we don't repeat it over and over.


EXAMPLE STORY (WITHOUT PRONOUNS)
-----------------------------------
Sarah is a chef at a famous restaurant. Sarah works with Mr. Kim and
Mr. Kim's daughter Lucy. Mr. Kim and Mr. Kim's daughter Lucy are
experts in French cuisine. Sarah, Mr. Kim, and Lucy created a new
dessert menu for the restaurant.

Notice how many times we repeat "Sarah," "Mr. Kim," and "Lucy"?
It sounds repetitive and clunky.


THE SAME STORY WITH PRONOUNS
--------------------------------
Sarah is a chef at a famous restaurant. She works with Mr. Kim and
his daughter Lucy. He and his daughter Lucy are experts in French
cuisine. They created a new dessert menu for the restaurant.

Much smoother! Pronouns save us from repeating the same names
again and again.


PERSONAL PRONOUNS
--------------------
Personal pronouns stand in for a specific person (or people).

  - I cook dinner every night.
  - You are a great singer.
  - They are from Brazil.
  - She teaches math.
  - We sent him a letter.

The word "it" stands in for a thing or object (not a person):

  - I ate it.
  - It is heavy.
  - They painted it blue.


PERSONAL PRONOUN CHART
--------------------------
                        1st Person   2nd Person   3rd Person
                                                   (Male) (Female) (Thing)
Singular - Subject         I            you         he     she     it
Singular - Object          me           you         him    her     it
Singular - Reflexive       myself       yourself    himself herself itself

Plural - Subject           we           you         they
Plural - Object            us           you         them
Plural - Reflexive         ourselves    yourselves  themselves


EASY WAY TO REMEMBER: SUBJECT VS. OBJECT
--------------------------------------------
  Subject pronoun = does the action    -> "She called him."
  Object pronoun  = receives the action -> "She called him." (him = object)

Trick: If the pronoun comes BEFORE the verb, it's a subject.
If it comes AFTER the verb (or after a preposition like "to," "with,"
"for"), it's usually an object.

  - He gave the book to her.      (He = subject, her = object)
  - We invited them to the party. (We = subject, them = object)


REFLEXIVE PRONOUNS - "SELF" WORDS
--------------------------------------
Use these when the subject and object are the SAME PERSON:

  - I hurt myself.
  - She made the cake herself.
  - They cleaned the house themselves.

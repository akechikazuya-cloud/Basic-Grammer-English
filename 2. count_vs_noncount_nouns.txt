COUNT NOUNS VS. NON-COUNT NOUNS
================================

COUNT NOUNS
-----------
These are things you can count one by one.

Examples:
apple, laptop, backpack, key, lamp, mug, phone, jacket, shoe, ring,
tree, guitar, umbrella, notebook, plate, bicycle

Add "-s" to make them plural:
apples, laptops, backpacks, keys, lamps, mugs, phones, jackets, shoes,
rings, trees, guitars, umbrellas, notebooks, plates, bicycles

They pair naturally with words like:
a few, few, many, some, every, each, these, the number of

  - a few apples
  - many laptops
  - some keys
  - every lamp
  - each mug
  - these phones
  - the number of jackets

They take articles (a / an / the):
  - a phone
  - an umbrella
  - the bicycle

They never pair with "much":
  X much laptops
  ✓ many laptops


NON-COUNT NOUNS
---------------
These describe things treated as a whole or a category — you don't
count them individually.

Examples:
coffee, sand, gold, smoke, French, Japanese, homework, furniture,
juice, butter, salt, corn, honey, tennis, weather

They usually don't have a plural form:
  X coffees (as a general concept)
  X furnitures

They can appear with or without an article, depending on the sentence:
  - Coffee is bitter.
  - The weather is lovely today.
  - I drink juice.
  - He plays tennis.
  - She loves French.
  - The sand is hot.

They pair with words like:
some, any, enough, this, that, much

  - We bought some sand and gold.
  - I hope to have enough coffee today.
  - This honey is delicious.
  - He doesn't do much homework.
  - Do you have any furniture?
  - That butter smells fresh.

They never pair with:
these, those, every, each, either, neither

  X these homework
  ✓ this homework


QUICK MEMORY TRICK FOR LEARNERS
--------------------------------
Ask yourself: "Can I put a number in front of it?"

  - "Two apples" -> YES -> count noun
  - "Two coffees" -> NO (unless you mean two cups/servings) -> non-count noun

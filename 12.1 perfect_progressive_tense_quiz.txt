PERFECT PROGRESSIVE TENSE QUIZ - PRACTICE EXERCISES
=====================================================
Instructions: All questions below are multiple choice, true/false, or 
matching. Just circle, underline, or write the letter/number of your 
answer. No full sentences needed!


SECTION 1: MULTIPLE CHOICE (PRESENT PERFECT PROGRESSIVE)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Choose the correct present perfect progressive form.

1. I ____ all morning, and the kitchen still smells amazing.
   a) cook            b) have been cooking       c) had been cooking

2. He ____ , and now he needs some water.
   a) has been jogging   b) jogs      c) had been jogging

3. They ____ the fence, and their hands are covered in paint.
   a) paint             b) have been painting      c) had been painting

4. We ____ for an hour, and we are getting impatient.
   a) wait               b) have been waiting       c) had been waiting


SECTION 2: MULTIPLE CHOICE (PAST PERFECT PROGRESSIVE)
----------------------------------------------------------------
Choose the correct past perfect progressive form.

5. I ____ all morning, and the kitchen still smelled amazing when 
   the guests arrived.
   a) have been cooking   b) had been cooking      c) cook

6. He ____ , and he still needed water when he got home.
   a) has been jogging    b) had been jogging      c) jogs

7. They ____ the fence when the rain started.
   a) have been painting   b) had been painting     c) paint

8. We ____ for an hour when the bus finally showed up.
   a) have been waiting    b) had been waiting      c) wait


SECTION 3: MULTIPLE CHOICE (FUTURE PERFECT PROGRESSIVE)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Choose the correct future perfect progressive form.

9. By tonight, I ____ for six hours straight.
   a) will have been cooking   b) have been cooking   c) had been cooking

10. By next month, he ____ every day for a whole year.
    a) will have been jogging  b) has been jogging    c) had been jogging

11. By the time the guests arrive, they ____ since early morning.
    a) will have been painting  b) have been painting  c) had been painting


SECTION 4: MATCHING (FORMULAS)
------------------------------------
Match each tense on the left with its correct formula on the right.

  1. Present Perfect Progressive     a) had + been + verb-ing
  2. Past Perfect Progressive        b) will + have + been + verb-ing
  3. Future Perfect Progressive      c) have/has + been + verb-ing


SECTION 5: TRUE OR FALSE
------------------------------
Read each sentence. Circle TRUE if the tense form is correct,

15. "By next summer, I will been running for a year."                  TRUE / FALSE
16. "They had been waiting when the bus arrived."                      TRUE / FALSE

SECTION 6: WHICH TENSE IS IT?
------------------------------------
Read each sentence and choose the correct tense name.

18. "We have been studying for two hours, and we're finally done."
    a) present perfect progressive    b) past perfect progressive    c) future perfect progressive

19. "She had been cleaning the house when the guests knocked."
    a) present perfect progressive    b) past perfect progressive    c) future perfect progressive

20. "By June, they will have been living here for ten years."
    a) present perfect progressive    b) past perfect progressive    c) future perfect progressive

21. "He has been reading that book all week."
    a) present perfect progressive    b) past perfect progressive    c) future perfect progressive

22. "By midnight, I will have been driving for eight hours."
    a) present perfect progressive    b) past perfect progressive    c) future perfect progressive

===================================
CAN/COULD AND MAY/MIGHT/MUST QUIZ - PRACTICE EXERCISES
===================================
Instructions: All questions below are multiple choice, true/false, or 
matching. Just circle, underline, or write the letter/number of your 
answer. No full sentences needed!


SECTION 1: MULTIPLE CHOICE ("CAN" USES)
----------------------------------------------------
Choose the correct use of "can" described for each sentence.

1. "I can bake a three-layer cake from scratch."
   a) ability        b) permission       c) request

2. "Can I borrow your charger?"
   a) ability        b) permission       c) request

3. "Can I get a refill, please?"
   a) ability        b) permission       c) request


SECTION 2: MULTIPLE CHOICE ("COULD" USES)
----------------------------------------------------
Choose the correct use of "could" described for each sentence.

4. "I could climb trees easily when I was a child."
   a) past ability      b) polite permission     c) possibility

5. "Could I borrow your pen for a moment?"
   a) past ability       b) polite permission     c) possibility

6. "This idea could actually succeed."
   a) past ability        b) polite permission     c) possibility


SECTION 3: MULTIPLE CHOICE ("MAY" USES)
----------------------------------------------------
Choose the correct use of "may" described for each sentence.

7. "May I enter the room?"
   a) formal permission     b) possibility

8. "It may snow later tonight."
   a) formal permission      b) possibility

9. "He may accept the job offer."
   a) formal permission      b) possibility


SECTION 4: MULTIPLE CHOICE ("MIGHT" VS. "MAY")
--------------------------------------------------------
Choose the correct word based on the level of certainty described.

10. Describing a smaller, more uncertain possibility:
    a) may       b) might

11. Which modal is used more often in everyday American English 
    to express uncertain possibility?
    a) may        b) might

12. "She ____ have already left for the airport." (uncertain guess)
    a) may        b) might


SECTION 5: MULTIPLE CHOICE ("MUST" USES)
----------------------------------------------------
Choose the correct use of "must" described for each sentence.

13. "Every driver must wear a seatbelt."
    a) necessity      b) strong certainty

14. "She must be exhausted after that long flight."
    a) necessity       b) strong certainty

15. "You must be kidding!"
    a) necessity        b) strong certainty

16. "This building must have an emergency exit."
    a) necessity         b) strong certainty


SECTION 6: MATCHING (MODAL VERB TO MAIN USE)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Match each modal verb on the left with its most common use 
on the right.

  1. Can           a) Formal permission or possibility
  2. Could          b) Ability or casual request
  3. May            c) Necessity or strong certainty
  4. Might          d) Past ability or polite permission
  5. Must           e) Smaller, uncertain possibility


SECTION 7: TRUE OR FALSE
------------------------------
Read each sentence. Circle TRUE if the modal verb form is correct, 
or FALSE if it is wrong.

17. "Can you open this jar?"                          TRUE / FALSE
18. "Could you please hold the elevator?"              TRUE / FALSE
19. "May I ask one question?"                          TRUE / FALSE
20. "He might have finished it."                       TRUE / FALSE
21. "Everyone must saves the natural resources."       TRUE / FALSE
22. "There must be some kind of misunderstanding."      TRUE / FALSE


SECTION 8: WHICH WORD FITS BEST?
------------------------------------------
Choose the modal verb that best completes each sentence.

23. ____ I ask a quick question? (formal permission)
    a) Can        b) May        c) Must

24. This shop ____ have closed early today. (uncertain guess)
    a) may         b) might       c) must

25. Citizens ____ pay their taxes on time. (strong requirement)
    a) can          b) might        c) must

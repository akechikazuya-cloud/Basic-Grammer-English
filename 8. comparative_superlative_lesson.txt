WHAT ARE COMPARATIVE AND SUPERLATIVE ADJECTIVES?
-----------------------------------------------------
When we compare TWO things, we use a COMPARATIVE adjective.
When we compare THREE OR MORE things (or pick the "number one" out of 
a whole group), we use a SUPERLATIVE adjective.

Think of it like a race:
  Comparative = comparing two runners: "Runner A is faster than Runner B."
  Superlative = picking the winner out of the whole race: "Runner A is 
  the fastest of all."


RULE 1: SHORT ADJECTIVES (ONE SYLLABLE) --> ADD "-ER" / "-EST"
--------------------------------------------------------------------
For short, one-syllable adjectives, simply add "-er" to compare two 
things, and "-est" to compare three or more.

  fast   --> faster   --> fastest
  small  --> smaller  --> smallest
  tall   --> taller   --> tallest
  cheap  --> cheaper  --> cheapest
  loud   --> louder   --> loudest
  cold   --> colder   --> coldest


RULE 2: ADJECTIVES ENDING IN "-Y" --> CHANGE "Y" TO "I"
-------------------------------------------------------------
If a short adjective ends in "y," swap the "y" for an "i" before 
adding "-er" or "-est."

  happy   --> happier   --> happiest
  busy    --> busier    --> busiest
  easy    --> easier    --> easiest
  heavy   --> heavier   --> heaviest
  funny   --> funnier   --> funniest

Memory trick: "Y feels shy around -er and -est, so it changes into I 
to fit in better."


RULE 3: LONGER ADJECTIVES (TWO OR MORE SYLLABLES) --> USE "MORE" / "MOST"
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
For longer adjectives, don't change the word itself. Instead, place 
"more" in front for comparatives, and "most" in front for superlatives.

  careful       --> more careful       --> most careful
  expensive     --> more expensive     --> most expensive
  intelligent   --> more intelligent   --> most intelligent
  generous      --> more generous     --> most generous
  exciting      --> more exciting     --> most exciting

Memory trick: "Long word, no room to squeeze in a suffix — so we add 
a helper word in front instead."


RULE 4: IRREGULAR ADJECTIVES (THEY BREAK THE RULES!)
----------------------------------------------------------
A few common adjectives don't follow any pattern at all. You simply 
have to memorize them, like sight words.

  good        --> better       --> best
  bad         --> worse        --> worst
  little      --> less         --> least
  much/many   --> more         --> most
  far         --> further      --> furthest

Memory trick: Think of these as the "rebel five" — they refuse to 
follow the rules, so give them extra practice.


RULE 5: USE "THAN" WHEN COMPARING TWO THINGS
--------------------------------------------------
Comparative sentences almost always include the word "than."

  My coffee is hotter than yours.
  Leo is braver than his younger brother.
  This backpack is more comfortable than that one.
  I have more free time than my coworker.
  She trusts him more than me.


RULE 6: USE "THE" WITH SUPERLATIVES
-----------------------------------------
Superlative sentences almost always include the word "the" right 
before the adjective.

  Mount Everest is the tallest mountain in the world.
  Mia is the fastest runner on the team.
  This is the most delicious cake I have ever tasted.
  That was the funniest movie I have ever seen.

PRACTICE TIP FOR LEARNERS
-----------------------------
Try comparing three objects near you right now — for example, three 
pens, three chairs, or three photos. Say one comparative sentence and 
one superlative sentence about them:

  This pen is longer than that one.
  This is the longest pen of the three.

  My chair is more comfortable than yours.
  This is the most comfortable chair in the room.

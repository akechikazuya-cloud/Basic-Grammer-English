WHAT IS VOICE IN A SENTENCE?
--------------------------------
Every sentence with a verb has a "voice" — either ACTIVE or PASSIVE. 
Voice tells us the relationship between the subject and the action.


ACTIVE VOICE: THE SUBJECT IS THE "DOER"
---------------------------------------------
In active voice, the subject performs the action directly. It's the 
simplest, most direct way to build a sentence — like a clear arrow 
pointing from the doer to the action.

  The chef prepared the meal.
  The teacher graded the essays.
  My neighbor planted a garden.
  The committee approved the proposal.

In each sentence, the subject (chef, teacher, neighbor, committee) is 
clearly the one DOING the action.


PASSIVE VOICE: THE SUBJECT RECEIVES THE ACTION
-----------------------------------------------------
In passive voice, the subject is not the doer — instead, the subject 
RECEIVES the action. The doer might be mentioned with "by," or might 
be left out completely.

  The meal was prepared. (by the chef — not mentioned)
  The essays were graded. (by the teacher — not mentioned)
  The garden was planted. (by my neighbor — not mentioned)
  The proposal was approved by the committee. (doer included)

Memory trick: In active voice, the subject "throws the ball." In 
passive voice, the subject "catches the ball" — the action happens 
TO it, not FROM it.


WHEN DO WE USE PASSIVE VOICE?
-----------------------------------
Passive voice is useful when the ACTION itself matters more than who 
did it — because the doer is unknown, unimportant, or obvious from 
context.

  The bridge is closed. 
  (We don't know or don't need to know who closed it.)

  My phone was stolen.
  (The focus is on the phone, not on finding the thief right now.)

  Dinner is served.
  (Nobody needs to know exactly who cooked it — the focus is dinner 
  being ready.)

Passive voice can also sound more polite or less accusatory, since it 
avoids pointing a direct finger at someone.

  Compare:
    "You made a mistake in the report." (active — sounds blunt)
    "A mistake was made in the report." (passive — softer tone)


MORE PASSIVE VOICE EXAMPLES
----------------------------------
  This app was designed in 2015.
  The telephone was invented over a century ago.
  I was informed that the flight was delayed.
  Your feedback is valued.
  He was awarded first prize.
  It was reported that the store would close early.
  It is suggested that the meeting be rescheduled.


HOW TO CHANGE ACTIVE VOICE INTO PASSIVE VOICE
-----------------------------------------------------
Follow these three simple steps:

  STEP 1: The OBJECT of the active sentence becomes the new SUBJECT.
  STEP 2: The verb changes to "be" verb + past participle.
  STEP 3: The original subject either follows "by" or is dropped 
  entirely.

Example 1:
  ACTIVE:   Maria baked a cake.
  PASSIVE:  A cake was baked by Maria.

Example 2:
  ACTIVE:   The city built a new library.
  PASSIVE:  A new library was built by the city.

Example 3:
  ACTIVE:   I suggest that you revise your essay.
  PASSIVE:  It is suggested that you revise your essay.

Memory trick: Picture flipping the sentence like a pancake — what was 
the object (the thing being acted upon) flips up to become the new 
subject, and the "doer" slides to the end (or disappears entirely).

PRACTICE TIP FOR LEARNERS
-----------------------------
Take an active sentence about your own life and try flipping it into 
passive voice using the three steps above:

  ACTIVE:  I cleaned the kitchen.
  PASSIVE: The kitchen was cleaned (by me).

  ACTIVE:  My boss praised my work.
  PASSIVE: My work was praised (by my boss).

Ask yourself: "Does this sentence sound better focusing on the doer, 
or on the action/result?" That will help you decide which voice fits 
best.

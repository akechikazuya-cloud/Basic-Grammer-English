INFINITIVES QUIZ - PRACTICE EXERCISES
===================================
Instructions: All questions below are multiple choice, true/false, or 
matching. Just circle, underline, or write the letter/number of your 
answer. No full sentences needed!


SECTION 1: MULTIPLE CHOICE (INFINITIVE AS OBJECT OF A VERB)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Choose the correct infinitive form to complete each sentence.

1. I want ____ next summer.
   a) travel        b) traveling       c) to travel

2. She hopes ____ this year.
   a) graduate      b) graduating      c) to graduate

3. They promised ____ us move.
   a) help          b) helping         c) to help

4. He decided ____ his career.
   a) change        b) changing        c) to change


SECTION 2: MULTIPLE CHOICE (INFINITIVE AS SUBJECT)
----------------------------------------------------------
Choose the correct infinitive form.

5. ____ the world is my biggest dream.
   a) Travel        b) Traveling       c) To travel

6. ____ someone is not always easy.
   a) Forgive       b) Forgiving       c) To forgive


SECTION 3: MULTIPLE CHOICE (SHOWING PURPOSE)
--------------------------------------------------
Choose the sentence where the infinitive correctly shows purpose 
(answers "why?").

7. Which sentence shows purpose?
   a) I like to read books.
   b) She woke up early to catch the sunrise.
   c) To read is relaxing.

8. Which sentence shows purpose?
   a) He studies every night to improve his grades.
   b) He wants to study tonight.
   c) Studying is important.


SECTION 4: MULTIPLE CHOICE (INFINITIVE DESCRIBING A NOUN)
------------------------------------------------------------------
Choose the correct infinitive form.

9. I have a decision ____.
   a) make          b) making          c) to make

10. This is the perfect time ____.
    a) start         b) starting        c) to start

11. She has a story ____.
    a) tell          b) telling         c) to tell


SECTION 5: TRUE OR FALSE (NEGATIVE INFINITIVES)
------------------------------------------------------
Read each sentence. Circle TRUE if the negative infinitive form is 
correct, or FALSE if it is wrong.

12. "Remember not to be late tomorrow."       TRUE / FALSE
13. "She decided to not attend the party."     TRUE / FALSE
14. "Try not to worry so much."                TRUE / FALSE
15. "He promised to not interrupt again."      TRUE / FALSE


SECTION 6: MATCHING (INFINITIVE ROLES)
------------------------------------------
Match each sentence on the left with the correct infinitive role 
on the right.

  1. "I want to learn."                a) Shows purpose
  2. "To forgive is healing."          b) Object of a verb
  3. "I saved money to travel."        c) Describes a noun
  4. "I have work to do."              d) Subject of a sentence


SECTION 7: MULTIPLE CHOICE (SPLIT INFINITIVES)
--------------------------------------------------
Choose the sentence that correctly shows a split infinitive.

16. a) I need you to focus really on this.
    b) I need you to really focus on this.
    c) I need you really to focus on this.

17. a) You have to truly believe in yourself.
    b) You have truly to believe in yourself.
    c) You have to believe truly in yourself.

19. Infinitives can never act as the subject of a sentence.  TRUE / FALSE
20. Infinitives can show the purpose or reason for an action. TRUE / FALSE
21. Split infinitives are always grammatically wrong.         TRUE / FALSE

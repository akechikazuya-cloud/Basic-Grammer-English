===================================
PREPOSITIONS: "OF," "TO," AND "FOR"
===================================

A QUICK REMINDER ABOUT PREPOSITIONS
------------------------------------------
Prepositions connect nouns and pronouns to the rest of a sentence, 
often showing ownership, direction, purpose, or time. There isn't 
always a strict logical rule — the best approach is to notice these 
words often in real sentences and build a feel for them over time.


THE BIG PICTURE: THREE DIFFERENT JOBS
--------------------------------------------
Think of these three prepositions as three different roles at a 
party:

  OF   = the "belongs to" word (showing connection or ownership)
  TO   = the "moving toward" word (showing direction or destination)
  FOR  = the "purpose" word (showing why or for whom)


PART 1: "OF" — BELONGING, REFERENCE, AND AMOUNTS
===================================

USE A: SHOWING OWNERSHIP OR CONNECTION
--------------------------------------------
  The ending of this story surprised everyone.
  The best part of the concert was the encore.
  The cover of that magazine is stunning.
  That bicycle belongs to the neighbor of mine.
  I always dreamed of running my own business.

USE B: SHOWING REFERENCE (POINTING TO SOMETHING SPECIFIC)
------------------------------------------------------------------
  We got married in the spring of 2010.
  This is a photo of my childhood home.
  I received a discount of 20 percent.

USE C: SHOWING AN AMOUNT OR QUANTITY
--------------------------------------------
  I drank two glasses of orange juice.
  A huge crowd of tourists visited the museum.
  I only had five hours of sleep last night.
  She earned a perfect score of 100 on the exam.

Memory trick: "Of" usually links two nouns together, showing that one 
belongs to, describes, or measures the other.


PART 2: "TO" — DIRECTION, LIMITS, RELATIONSHIP, AND TIME
===================================

USE A: SHOWING MOVEMENT TOWARD A PLACE, PERSON, OR THING
------------------------------------------------------------------
  I am walking to the front gate of the building.
  The letter was sent to my cousin last week.
  We all drove to the amusement park.
  Please forward this message to her.

USE B: SHOWING A LIMIT OR ENDING POINT
--------------------------------------------
  The water rose up to the windowsill.
  Ticket prices climbed up to 150 dollars.

USE C: SHOWING A RELATIONSHIP OR CONNECTION BETWEEN IDEAS
------------------------------------------------------------------
  This detail is crucial to your final grade.
  My response to your email is attached below.
  Try not to react to every small inconvenience.

USE D: SHOWING TIME OR A PERIOD
--------------------------------------
  I work eight to four, Tuesday to Saturday.
  It's now five to nine. (In other words, it's 8:55.)

Memory trick: "To" almost always points forward — toward a 
destination, a deadline, a person, or a limit.


PART 3: "FOR" — PURPOSE, REASON, AND DURATION
===================================

USE A: SHOWING THE PURPOSE OR USE OF SOMETHING
--------------------------------------------------------
  This room is for meetings and workshops.
  I baked cookies for the school fundraiser.
  She left a note on the fridge for reminders.
  He has been training hard for the tournament.

USE B: SHOWING REASON ("BECAUSE OF")
--------------------------------------------
  I am so proud of you.
  We feel terrible for your loss.
  For that reason, I decided to change my major.

USE C: SHOWING TIME OR DURATION
--------------------------------------
  She's been a teacher for over twenty years.
  I lived in that city for six months only.
  That's everything I have for today.

Memory trick: "For" usually answers the question "why?" or "how 
long?" — it's the word of purpose and duration.


QUICK REFERENCE CHART
------------------------------------------------------------
 PREPOSITION | USED FOR                          | EXAMPLE
------------------------------------------------------------
 Of          | Belonging, reference, amount        | a cup of tea
 To          | Direction, limit, relationship, time | go to the park
 For         | Purpose, reason, duration             | a gift for you
------------------------------------------------------------


PRACTICE TIP FOR LEARNERS
-----------------------------
Try building one sentence with each preposition about your own life:

  OF: The best part ____ my day was ____.
  TO: I am going ____ ____ later today.
  FOR: I am grateful ____ ____.

The more often you notice and practice these small connector words, 
the more naturally they will start to feel in conversation.

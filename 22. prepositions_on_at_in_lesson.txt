===================================
PREPOSITIONS: "ON," "AT," AND "IN"
===================================

WHAT IS A PREPOSITION?
---------------------------
A preposition is a small connector word that links a noun or pronoun 
to the rest of the sentence. It usually shows LOCATION, TIME, or 
DIRECTION.

  on the shelf
  at the corner
  in the box
  by noon
  under the bridge

Fair warning: prepositions don't always follow a clear, logical rule 
— sometimes you just have to get used to hearing them in context. The 
best strategy is to notice them often in reading and listening, and 
slowly build a natural feel for which one "sounds right."


THE BIG PICTURE: THINK OF A DARTBOARD
-------------------------------------------
A simple way to picture "on," "at," and "in" is to imagine zooming in 
and out on a map, like a dartboard with rings:

  IN   = the biggest ring (inside a large space or general area)
  ON   = a flat surface (touching the top of something)
  AT   = a specific point (a precise dot on the map)


PART 1: "ON" — SURFACES, DAYS, DEVICES, BODY PARTS, AND STATES
===================================

USE A: A SURFACE (TOUCHING THE TOP OF SOMETHING)
--------------------------------------------------------
  I placed the vase on the windowsill.
  The remote control is on the couch.
  There's a spider on the ceiling!

USE B: DAYS AND DATES
----------------------------
  The mail arrives on Tuesdays.
  She was born on the 3rd of March in 1995.
  Our meeting is on the 10th.

USE C: DEVICES OR MACHINES (PHONE, COMPUTER, TV)
--------------------------------------------------------
  He has been on the phone for an hour.
  I've been on my laptop all morning.
  There's a great documentary on TV tonight.

USE D: PARTS OF THE BODY
-------------------------------
  The ball hit her on the arm.
  He patted me on the back.
  She wears a bracelet on her wrist.

USE E: A STATE OR CONDITION
----------------------------------
  Everything in the shop is on discount.
  The warehouse is on lockdown.
  The stove is on fire!


PART 2: "AT" — SPECIFIC TIMES, PLACES, EMAILS, AND ACTIVITIES
===================================

USE A: A SPECIFIC TIME
------------------------------
  Let's meet at 3 p.m.
  The train departs at 7:15 sharp.

USE B: A SPECIFIC PLACE OR POINT
----------------------------------------
  There's a concert at the town hall.
  Hundreds of fans gathered at the arena.
  We watched fireworks at the harbor.

USE C: AN EMAIL ADDRESS
------------------------------
  You can reach me at hello@example.com.

USE D: AN ACTIVITY OR SKILL
------------------------------------
  She laughed at his terrible joke.
  I'm quite good at solving puzzles.
  He's excellent at chess.

Memory trick: "At" points to one exact spot, moment, or activity — 
like putting a pin directly on a dot.


PART 3: "IN" — GENERAL TIMES, LARGER SPACES, DESCRIPTIONS, AND FEELINGS
===================================

USE A: GENERAL OR UNSPECIFIC TIMES
------------------------------------------
  I usually check my email in the morning.
  In the winter, this town gets very quiet.
  The festival will take place in October.

USE B: A LOCATION OR LARGER SPACE
------------------------------------------
  She looked me straight in the eye.
  We're staying in a small cabin this weekend.
  My hometown is Austin, which is in Texas.

USE C: SHAPE, COLOR, OR SIZE
------------------------------------
  This scarf comes mostly in green.
  The dancers formed a line.
  This jacket is available in three sizes.

USE D: WHILE DOING SOMETHING (AN ONGOING PROCESS)
--------------------------------------------------------
  In preparing the presentation, we rehearsed several times.
  A strong headline is essential in advertising a new product.

USE E: BELIEFS, OPINIONS, OR FEELINGS
--------------------------------------------
  I believe in second chances.
  We are not interested in joining that club.

Memory trick: "In" wraps around something bigger — a whole month, a 
whole city, a whole idea, or a whole feeling.


QUICK REFERENCE CHART
------------------------------------------------------------
 PREPOSITION | USED FOR                          | EXAMPLE
------------------------------------------------------------
 On          | Surfaces, days, devices, body      | on the table
 At          | Exact time, place, activity          | at 5 p.m.
 In          | General time, larger space, feeling   | in June

PRACTICE TIP FOR LEARNERS
-----------------------------
Picture the "dartboard" idea again: IN is the wide outer ring, ON is 
the flat surface layer, and AT is the tiny bullseye dot. Try 
describing your weekend plans using all three:

  IN the evening, I'll relax at home.
  ON Saturday, I have a picnic planned.
  I'll meet my friends AT the park entrance.

The more sentences you build using these three prepositions, the more 
naturally they will start to feel.
